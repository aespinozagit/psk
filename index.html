Our Story

The Beginning...

Pearl Snap Kolaches (named for the famous pearl snap button, western style shirts) was started in Fort Worth by two friends, Wade and Greg, who were unsatisfied with the type of kolaches they could find locally. Neither one of them knew anything about baking or about how to make kolaches – they just knew they loved eating them!

For over a year, they tried dozens of recipes and traveled all across Texas talking to the experts and learning the craft of making amazing kolaches. Eventually, they created their own secret bread recipe that soon won over friends and family alike.

Greg and Wade believe that if you make a quality product at a fair price and back it up with great service – first time customers will become repeat customers. Their dream is for friends and families to make Pearl Snap Kolaches part of their morning ritual for years to come!

What Is A Kolache?

A Kolache (pronounced KO-LA-CHEE) is a Czech breakfast pastry made from sweet-bread, and is eaten by hand like doughnut or Danish. Traditional Kolaches are filled with fruit or cream cheese. Traditional Klobasniky (pronounced KLO-BAAS-NICKY) are made from the same sweet bread but filled with sausage and cheese. At PSK, we use the term “Kolache” to mean either of these two delicious morsels. Over the centuries in Texas, Kolaches have become part of our heritage and evolved to include influences from an array of cultures. Today kolaches truly are the Texan alternative to bagels, doughnuts and other breakfast choices. They are perfect for large groups at office meetings, sports teams and school events. They are also amazing while enjoying some of our gourmet roasted coffee and reading the morning paper.
